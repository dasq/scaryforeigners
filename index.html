Scary Foreigners

* * *

Scary Foreigners are a Dresden-based 4-piece band hailing from three different countries. With the combination of Scottish, Algerian/French, and American backgrounds, Scary Foreigners cover a range of tunes and styles, and add political humor as well, proving that foreigners aren’t really scary at all.

Greg Davis was born in Oxford, England, and raised in North Carolina, USA. The product of a musical family, he began playing the violin at age 5 and decided at age 13 that the electric guitar was a much cooler instrument. First rock music, then onto jazz which he studied at the University of North Carolina at Wilmington. Jazz combos, jazz composition and arranging, and three years in a 20-piece big band that toured Europe, including a gig at the North Sea jazz festival. This pivotal experience eventually resulted in his decision to move to Europe. The piano came into the picture in the mid ‘90s, and after 6 years of touring in a progressive rock band, a few tours with Claude Coleman Jr. of Ween, and stints in various other groups, he was ready to offer his strangely appropriate musical outbursts to Scary Foreigners. Greg also plays various instruments in other local Dresden bands including The Voltz Brothers, Seau Volant, and the Slingshots.

Tim Cross is from (long story short) Fairbanks, Alaska. After dropping out of university he spent several years wandering and doing odd jobs throughout western North America from Prudhoe Bay to Oaxaca, and settled in Denver in 1985, where he started studying contrabass, under the tutelage of the venerable and hilarious Mary Stribling. After moving to Boulder in 1989, he performed and recorded with Julie Hoest, played in the Longmont Symphony for a bit, was a founding member of Lauging Hands and Girls On Top!, and played in a number of short-lived jazz ensembles. In 2001 he moved to Dresden. Since then he's played with the New Town Swing Orchestra, Pangaea Trio, Hot Buttered Grits, Mongrel Blues Band, Quick & Dirty, and Enough Rope. He still performs with Unlimited Blues, Lutz Kowalewski and the Red Rugs, and the Voltz Brothers. He and his wife have two cats.
